The Sound of Silence

Associated with Simon & Garfunkel · 1964

Paul Simon wrote "The Sound of Silence" at 21, working in a darkened bathroom whose tiled acoustics he liked. The original acoustic version, recorded in 1964, sold poorly until producer Tom Wilson overdubbed electric instruments without the duo's knowledge in 1965, turning it into a No. 1 hit.
