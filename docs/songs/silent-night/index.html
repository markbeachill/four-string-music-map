Silent Night

Associated with Traditional (Franz Gruber)

Joseph Mohr wrote the words to "Silent Night" in 1816 and asked schoolteacher Franz Xaver Gruber to set them to music for guitar in time for Christmas Eve Mass in 1818 at a church in Oberndorf, Austria. Now translated into hundreds of languages, it is one of the most recorded songs in history and was named UNESCO intangible cultural heritage in 2011.
