Auld Lang Syne

Associated with Traditional (Robert Burns)

Robert Burns sent "Auld Lang Syne" to the Scots Musical Museum in 1788, saying he took it down from an old man's singing rather than writing it himself. Its familiar tune, adapted from a melody by English composer William Shield, was paired with Burns's words in 1799, and bandleader Guy Lombardo's annual broadcasts later made it a New Year's Eve standard in North America.
